# Measurement of AC Power and Power Factor

1. **What is AC power?**  
   **Answer:** AC power is the rate at which electrical energy is consumed or supplied in an alternating current circuit.

2. **What is power factor?**  
   **Answer:** Power factor is the cosine of the phase angle between voltage and current.

3. **Write the formula for power factor.**  
   **Answer:** \( PF = \cos \phi \)

4. **What is apparent power?**  
   **Answer:** Apparent power is the product of RMS voltage and RMS current.

5. **Write the formula for apparent power.**  
   **Answer:** \( S = VI \)

6. **What is true power or active power?**  
   **Answer:** True power is the actual power consumed by the load.

7. **Write the formula for true power.**  
   **Answer:** \( P = VI\cos\phi \)

8. **What is reactive power?**  
   **Answer:** Reactive power is the power that alternately stores and returns energy in inductive and capacitive circuits.

9. **Write the formula for reactive power.**  
   **Answer:** \( Q = VI\sin\phi \)

10. **What is the SI unit of true power?**  
    **Answer:** Watt (W).

11. **What is the unit of apparent power?**  
    **Answer:** Volt-Ampere (VA).

12. **What is the unit of reactive power?**  
    **Answer:** Volt-Ampere Reactive (VAR).

13. **What instrument is used to measure voltage?**  
    **Answer:** Voltmeter.

14. **What instrument is used to measure current?**  
    **Answer:** Ammeter.

15. **What instrument is used to measure power directly?**  
    **Answer:** Wattmeter.

16. **How is a voltmeter connected in a circuit?**  
    **Answer:** In parallel with the load.

17. **How is an ammeter connected in a circuit?**  
    **Answer:** In series with the load.

18. **What does a wattmeter measure?**  
    **Answer:** Active or true power.

19. **What is the purpose of this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** To measure single-phase AC power and determine the power factor.

20. **What is meant by RMS value?**  
    **Answer:** The effective value of an alternating quantity equivalent to a DC value producing the same heating effect.

21. **What is the phase angle in an AC circuit?**  
    **Answer:** The angle between voltage and current waveforms.

22. **What is the power factor of a purely resistive load?**  
    **Answer:** Unity (1).

23. **Why is the power factor of a lamp load approximately unity?**  
    **Answer:** Because a lamp behaves mainly as a resistive load.

24. **What is a single-phase AC circuit?**  
    **Answer:** A circuit supplied by a single alternating voltage source.

25. **Why is power factor important?**  
    **Answer:** It indicates how effectively electrical power is being utilized.

26. **What does a low power factor indicate?**  
    **Answer:** Poor utilization of electrical power.

27. **What does a unity power factor indicate?**  
    **Answer:** All supplied power is converted into useful work.

28. **What causes a low power factor?**  
    **Answer:** Inductive or capacitive loads causing phase difference between voltage and current.

29. **What is meant by leading power factor?**  
    **Answer:** Current leads voltage in the circuit.

30. **What is meant by lagging power factor?**  
    **Answer:** Current lags behind voltage in the circuit.

31. **What is the relationship between apparent power, true power, and power factor?**  
    **Answer:** Power factor = True Power / Apparent Power.

32. **What is the function of an autotransformer?**  
    **Answer:** To provide a variable AC voltage output.

33. **Why is an autotransformer used in this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** To vary the applied voltage to the load.

34. **What is a resistive load?**  
    **Answer:** A load that consumes electrical energy without significant phase difference between voltage and current.

35. **What is an inductive load?**  
    **Answer:** A load in which current lags behind voltage.

36. **What is a capacitive load?**  
    **Answer:** A load in which current leads voltage.

37. **Why are multiple readings taken in this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** To analyze the variation of power and power factor under different operating conditions.

38. **What precaution should be taken before energizing the circuit?**  
    **Answer:** Ensure all connections are correct and secure.

39. **Why should measuring instruments be connected correctly?**  
    **Answer:** To obtain accurate readings and avoid damage to the instruments.

40. **What type of error may occur in power measurements?**  
    **Answer:** Instrumental and observational errors.

41. **Why is AC power measurement important?**  
    **Answer:** To determine energy consumption and circuit performance.

42. **What is the significance of power factor correction?**  
    **Answer:** It improves system efficiency and reduces power losses.

43. **How can power factor be improved?**  
    **Answer:** By using capacitors or power factor correction equipment.

44. **What is the difference between AC and DC power measurement?**  
    **Answer:** AC power depends on voltage, current, and phase angle, whereas DC power depends only on voltage and current.

45. **What conclusion is obtained from this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** AC power and power factor can be determined using voltmeter and ammeter readings and verified using a wattmeter.
