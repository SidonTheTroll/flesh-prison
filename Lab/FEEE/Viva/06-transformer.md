# Transformation Ratio Test on a Single-Phase Transformer

1. **What is a transformer?**  
   **Answer:** A transformer is a static electrical device that transfers electrical energy from one circuit to another through electromagnetic induction.

2. **What is the main purpose of a transformer?**  
   **Answer:** To increase or decrease AC voltage levels without changing the frequency.

3. **What is electromagnetic induction?**  
   **Answer:** The process by which a changing magnetic flux induces an EMF in a conductor.

4. **What are the main parts of a transformer?**  
   **Answer:** Primary winding, secondary winding, and magnetic core.

5. **What is the primary winding?**  
   **Answer:** The winding connected to the AC supply source.

6. **What is the secondary winding?**  
   **Answer:** The winding from which output voltage is obtained.

7. **What is the function of the magnetic core?**  
   **Answer:** To provide a low-reluctance path for magnetic flux.

8. **What type of supply is required for a transformer?**  
   **Answer:** Alternating current (AC) supply.

9. **Can a transformer operate on DC supply?**  
   **Answer:** No, because DC does not produce a changing magnetic flux.

10. **What is meant by transformation ratio?**  
    **Answer:** The ratio of secondary voltage to primary voltage of a transformer.

11. **Write the transformation ratio equation.**  
    **Answer:** \( \frac{V_2}{V_1} = \frac{N_2}{N_1} = k \)

12. **What does \(k\) represent in a transformer?**  
    **Answer:** The transformation ratio.

13. **What is a step-up transformer?**  
    **Answer:** A transformer whose secondary voltage is greater than the primary voltage.

14. **What is a step-down transformer?**  
    **Answer:** A transformer whose secondary voltage is lower than the primary voltage.

15. **What is the value of transformation ratio for a step-down transformer?**  
    **Answer:** Less than one.

16. **What is the value of transformation ratio for a step-up transformer?**  
    **Answer:** Greater than one.

17. **What is the purpose of the transformation ratio test?**  
    **Answer:** To verify the voltage ratio between the primary and secondary windings.

18. **Why is an autotransformer used in this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** To vary the input voltage supplied to the transformer.

19. **What is an autotransformer?**  
    **Answer:** A transformer having a single winding that acts as both primary and secondary.

20. **What instrument is used to measure voltage?**  
    **Answer:** Voltmeter.

21. **How is a voltmeter connected in a circuit?**  
    **Answer:** In parallel with the component across which voltage is measured.

22. **Why are voltmeters connected on both primary and secondary sides?**  
    **Answer:** To measure the input and output voltages of the transformer.

23. **What is meant by induced EMF?**  
    **Answer:** The voltage produced due to electromagnetic induction.

24. **What is magnetic flux?**  
    **Answer:** The total magnetic field passing through a given area.

25. **What is mutual induction?**  
    **Answer:** The induction of EMF in one coil due to the changing current in another coil.

26. **What is the SI unit of voltage?**  
    **Answer:** Volt (V).

27. **What is the SI unit of magnetic flux?**  
    **Answer:** Weber (Wb).

28. **Why is a laminated core used in transformers?**  
    **Answer:** To reduce eddy current losses.

29. **What are eddy current losses?**  
    **Answer:** Losses caused by circulating currents induced in the transformer core.

30. **What are hysteresis losses?**  
    **Answer:** Losses due to repeated magnetization and demagnetization of the core.

31. **What are core losses in a transformer?**  
    **Answer:** The sum of hysteresis and eddy current losses.

32. **What are copper losses in a transformer?**  
    **Answer:** Losses due to resistance of the windings.

33. **Why is AC necessary for transformer operation?**  
    **Answer:** Because AC produces a continuously changing magnetic flux.

34. **What happens to the frequency in a transformer?**  
    **Answer:** The frequency remains unchanged.

35. **What is meant by turns ratio?**  
    **Answer:** The ratio of secondary turns to primary turns.

36. **How is voltage related to the number of turns in a transformer?**  
    **Answer:** Voltage is directly proportional to the number of turns.

37. **What type of transformer is commonly used for domestic power distribution?**  
    **Answer:** Step-down transformer.

38. **What is the significance of the transformation ratio?**  
    **Answer:** It indicates how much the voltage is increased or decreased.

39. **What is meant by an ideal transformer?**  
    **Answer:** A transformer with no losses and 100% efficiency.

40. **What is transformer efficiency?**  
    **Answer:** The ratio of output power to input power.

41. **Why should all connections be checked before switching on the supply?**  
    **Answer:** To avoid incorrect readings and equipment damage.

42. **What type of error may occur in this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** Instrumental and observational errors.

43. **Why are multiple readings taken during the test?**  
    **Answer:** To verify the transformation ratio under different input voltages.

44. **What is the function of the primary winding?**  
    **Answer:** To receive electrical energy from the supply.

45. **What is the function of the secondary winding?**  
    **Answer:** To deliver electrical energy to the load.

46. **What conclusion is obtained from this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** The secondary voltage varies proportionally with the primary voltage, confirming the transformation ratio of the transformer.
