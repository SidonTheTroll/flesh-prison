# Measurement of Resistance by Ammeter and Voltmeter

1. **What is the objective of this experiment?**  
   **Answer:** To verify Ohm's law and determine the value of an unknown resistance.

2. **State Ohm's Law.**  
   **Answer:** At constant temperature, the current through a conductor is directly proportional to the applied voltage and inversely proportional to its resistance.

3. **Write the mathematical expression of Ohm's Law.**  
   **Answer:** $( V = IR )$

4. **How can resistance be calculated using Ohm's Law?**  
   **Answer:** $( R = \frac{V}{I} )$

5. **What is the SI unit of resistance?**  
   **Answer:** Ohm (Ω).

6. **What is the SI unit of current?**  
   **Answer:** Ampere (A).

7. **What is the SI unit of voltage?**  
   **Answer:** Volt (V).

8. **What instrument is used to measure current?**  
   **Answer:** Ammeter.

9. **What instrument is used to measure voltage?**  
   **Answer:** Voltmeter.

10. **How is an ammeter connected in a circuit?**  
    **Answer:** In series with the load.

11. **How is a voltmeter connected in a circuit?**  
    **Answer:** In parallel across the load.

12. **Why is an ammeter connected in series?**  
    **Answer:** To measure the same current flowing through the circuit.

13. **Why is a voltmeter connected in parallel?**  
    **Answer:** To measure the potential difference across the component.

14. **What is the function of a rheostat?**  
    **Answer:** To vary the current in the circuit by changing resistance.

15. **What is meant by electrical resistance?**  
    **Answer:** The opposition offered by a material to the flow of electric current.

16. **What is conductance?**  
    **Answer:** The reciprocal of resistance.

17. **What is the SI unit of conductance?**  
    **Answer:** Siemens (S).

18. **What happens to current when voltage increases and resistance remains constant?**  
    **Answer:** Current increases proportionally.

19. **What happens to current when resistance increases and voltage remains constant?**  
    **Answer:** Current decreases.

20. **Why is Ohm's law important?**  
    **Answer:** It helps determine the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in electrical circuits.

21. **What is meant by a DC supply?**  
    **Answer:** A source that provides current in only one direction.

22. **What is the purpose of verifying Ohm's law experimentally?**  
    **Answer:** To confirm the linear relationship between voltage and current.

23. **What graph is generally plotted in this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** Voltage versus current (V-I graph).

24. **What is the nature of the V-I graph for an ohmic conductor?**  
    **Answer:** A straight line passing through the origin.

25. **What does the slope of the V-I graph represent?**  
    **Answer:** Resistance.

26. **What is meant by an ohmic conductor?**  
    **Answer:** A conductor that obeys Ohm's law.

27. **Give an example of an ohmic conductor.**  
    **Answer:** Metallic resistor.

28. **What are non-ohmic devices?**  
    **Answer:** Devices that do not obey Ohm's law.

29. **Give an example of a non-ohmic device.**  
    **Answer:** Diode.

30. **Why should connections be tight in the circuit?**  
    **Answer:** To avoid contact resistance and inaccurate readings.

31. **What causes instrumental error?**  
    **Answer:** Imperfections or limitations in measuring instruments.

32. **Why might the calculated resistance values differ slightly?**  
    **Answer:** Due to instrumental and observational errors.

33. **What precaution should be taken while connecting the circuit?**  
    **Answer:** Ensure correct polarity and proper connections before switching on the supply.

34. **Why is the rheostat adjusted during the experiment?**  
    **Answer:** To obtain different values of current and voltage.

35. **What conclusion is obtained from this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** The ratio of voltage to current remains nearly constant, verifying Ohm's law and giving the resistance value of the conductor.
