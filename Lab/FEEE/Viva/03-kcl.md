# Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)

1. **What is Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)?**  
   **Answer:** Kirchhoff's Current Law states that the algebraic sum of currents at a junction is zero.

2. **State KCL in simple words.**  
   **Answer:** The total current entering a junction is equal to the total current leaving the junction.

3. **On which principle is KCL based?**  
   **Answer:** The principle of conservation of charge.

4. **What is meant by conservation of charge?**  
   **Answer:** Electric charge can neither be created nor destroyed.

5. **Why is accumulation of charge at a junction not possible?**  
   **Answer:** Because the charge entering the junction is equal to the charge leaving it.

6. **What is a junction in an electrical circuit?**  
   **Answer:** A point where three or more conductors meet.

7. **What is a node in an electrical circuit?**  
   **Answer:** A point where two or more circuit elements are connected.

8. **What is the mathematical expression of KCL?**  
   **Answer:** \( \sum I = 0 \)

9. **How can KCL be expressed for incoming and outgoing currents?**  
   **Answer:** Sum of incoming currents = Sum of outgoing currents.

10. **What type of supply is used in this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** DC supply.

11. **What is the purpose of verifying KCL experimentally?**  
    **Answer:** To prove that the total current entering a junction equals the total current leaving it.

12. **What instrument is used to measure current?**  
    **Answer:** Ammeter.

13. **How is an ammeter connected in a circuit?**  
    **Answer:** In series.

14. **Why is an ammeter connected in series?**  
    **Answer:** To measure the current flowing through the circuit branch.

15. **What is the SI unit of current?**  
    **Answer:** Ampere (A).

16. **What is the function of a rheostat?**  
    **Answer:** To vary the current in the circuit.

17. **What is a potential divider?**  
    **Answer:** A circuit used to obtain different voltage levels from a source.

18. **Why are rheostats used in this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** To vary branch currents and verify KCL under different conditions.

19. **What is meant by branch current?**  
    **Answer:** The current flowing through an individual branch of a circuit.

20. **What is an electrical network?**  
    **Answer:** An interconnection of electrical components.

21. **What is an electrical circuit?**  
    **Answer:** A closed conducting path through which current flows.

22. **What happens if the incoming current is greater than the outgoing current at a junction?**  
    **Answer:** It would violate the law of conservation of charge.

23. **Can KCL be applied to AC circuits?**  
    **Answer:** Yes, KCL is applicable to both AC and DC circuits.

24. **Can KCL be applied to any junction in a circuit?**  
    **Answer:** Yes, provided current directions are properly considered.

25. **Why are current directions important in KCL?**  
    **Answer:** Because entering and leaving currents must be assigned proper signs.

26. **What is meant by algebraic sum of currents?**  
    **Answer:** The sum obtained by considering the sign of each current according to its direction.

27. **What type of error may affect KCL verification?**  
    **Answer:** Instrumental and observational errors.

28. **Why should circuit connections be tight?**  
    **Answer:** To avoid contact resistance and incorrect readings.

29. **What precaution should be taken before switching on the supply?**  
    **Answer:** Ensure all connections are correct and secure.

30. **Why are multiple readings taken in the experiment?**  
    **Answer:** To improve accuracy and confirm the validity of KCL.

31. **What is the significance of KCL in circuit analysis?**  
    **Answer:** It helps determine unknown currents in electrical networks.

32. **What is the difference between KCL and Ohm's Law?**  
    **Answer:** KCL relates currents at a junction, while Ohm's Law relates voltage, current, and resistance.

33. **Can KCL be used with Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)?**  
    **Answer:** Yes, both laws are commonly used together for circuit analysis.

34. **What is Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)?**  
    **Answer:** The algebraic sum of all voltages around a closed loop is zero.

35. **What conclusion is obtained from this experiment?**  
    **Answer:** The total current entering a junction is equal to the total current leaving the junction, thereby verifying Kirchhoff's Current Law.
