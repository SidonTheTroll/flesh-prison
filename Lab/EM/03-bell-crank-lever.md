- **Experiment no.:** 03 
- **Title of the Experiment:** Bell Crank Lever 
- **Object of the Experiment:** To verify the principle of moments by means of Bell Crank Lever 

## Theory 
The principle of moments states that the algebraic sum of moments of a system of coplanar forces about a point which are in equilibrium is zero, that is, sum of clockwise moments about a point is equal to the sum of anti-clockwise moments about the same point. 

In a bell crank lever, the clockwise moment about the fulcrum $(F = W \times L)$ and the anti-clockwise moment about the fulcrum $(F = P \times H)$, 

$$
\therefore P \times h =  W \times L
$$

## Apparatus Used 
Bell crank lever, meter scale, spring balance and weights. 

## Procedure 
1. The original reading of the spring balance ($P_1$) is noted down. 
2. A known weight $(W)$ is placed on the load arms at a known distance $(L)$ from the fulcrum. 
3. The final reading of the spring balance $(P_2)$ is noted down. The difference between $P_2$ and $P_1$ gives the effort P. 
4. The distance of the effort $(h)$ from the fulcrum is measured by means of meter scale. 
5. The clockwise moment $(W \times L)$ and the anti-clockwise moment $(P \times h)$ are now calculated and verified. 
6. The above procedures are repeated thrice for different value of weights.
