- **Experiment no.:** 07 
- **Title of the Expirment:** Simple Wheel and Axle
- **Object of the Experiment;** To determine the mechanical advantage velocity ratio, efficiency and machine friction of simple wheel and axle. 

## Apparatus Used 
Simple wheel and axle, weight box, meter scale 

## Theory 
The mechanical advantage (MA) of a machine has been defined as ratio of load lifted (W) to the effort applied (F)

$$
MA = \frac{W}{P}
$$

The velocity ratio (VR) of a simple wheel and axle is given by - 

$$
VR = \frac{D + d_1}{d + d_2} 
$$

Where,  
$D$ = diameter of wheel in cm  
$d$ = diameter of axle in cm 
$d_1$ = diameter of rope around the wheel in cm 
$d_2$ = diameter of rope round the axle in cm 

The efficiency $(\eta)$ of the machine is the ratio of the mechanical advantages and the velocity ratio and is always expressed in percentage. 

$$
\eta = \frac{MA}{VR} \times 100%
$$

The friction of the machine $F = (P \times VR) W$

## Procedure 
1. The circumference of the brake wheel (D) is found out by winding the rope round it and measuring the length by meter scale from which the diameter of the effort wheel is found. 
2. Similarly the diameter of the axle 'd' is found out by rope. 
3. The diameter of the rope $(d_1)$ around the wheel and diameter of the rope $(d_2)$ around the axle are found out by screw gauge/vernier caliper by taking average of at least four reading at different lengths. 
4. A known weight (W) is hung at the end of the rope winding it a number of turns around the axle. 
5. The other rope around the effort wheel is due to opposite direction to that of the axle at one end of which a scale pan is attached. 
6. Weight are gradually put on the pan till the load (W) at the end of the rope around the axle is just at the point of lifting the effort 'P' is noted down. 
7. The above procedure are repeated thrice by hanging different weight at the end of the rope around the axle. 

## Observation and Result 
The velocity ratio $(VR)$ of a simple wheel and axle is given by $VR = \frac{D + d_1}{d + d_2}$

- Circumference of the wheel, $\pi D = 114$ cm
- Circumference of the axle, $\pi d = 35$ cm
- Diameter of axle, $d = \frac{\pi D}{\pi} = 35/\pi = 11.15$ cm 
- Diameter of the wheel, $D = \frac{\pi D}{\pi} = 114/\pi - 36.3$ cm 
- Diameter of the rope around the wheel, $d_1 = 0.4$ cm 
- Diameter of the rope around the axle, $d_2 = 0.4$ cm 

$$
VR = \frac{D + d_1}{d + d_2} = \frac{36.3 + 0.4}{11.15 + 0.4} = 3.17 
$$
