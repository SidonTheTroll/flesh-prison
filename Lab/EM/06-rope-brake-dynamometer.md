- **Experiment no:** 06
- **Title of Experiment:** Rope Brake Dynamometer
- **Object of Experiment:** To find brake horse power by means of rope brake dynamometer. 

## Description of Rope Brake Dynamometer 
It consists of a rope which wraps over the rim of the brake wheel. One end of rope is attached to a spring balance 'S' and the other end carries a load 'W'. 

## Theory 
- Effective load over the rim of the wheel = $(W \sim S)$ kg 
- Effective circumference of the brake wheel, $\pi D$ = $\_\_\_\_$ m 
- Diameter of the brake wheel, D = $\frac{\text{Circumference}}{\pi}\ m$
- Diameter of the rope, d = $\_\_\_\_$ m 
- Effective radius of the wheel, R = $\frac{D+d}{2}$ m 
- Work done per revolution = $(W \sim S) \times 2\pi R$ kg-m 
- Work done per minute = $(W \sim S) \times 2\pi RN$ kg-m 
- Being the speed of the wheel is in RPM, BPH = $\frac{(W \sim S) \times 2\pi RN}{4500}$ HP = $\frac{2\pi NT}{4500}$ HP, where T = $(W \sim S) \times R$

## Procedure 
1. The circumference of the brake wheel is found out by winding the rope around it and measuring the rope by means of a meter scale from which the diameter $D$ is found out. 
2. The diameter of rope $d$ is found out by screw gauge/slide caliper.
3. The weight $'W'$ is hanging at the end of the rope whose other end is attached to a spring balance.
4. The wheel is rotated uniformly by means of handles for a particular number of revolutions $n$ and the time taken $t$ for these revolutions are noted by the stop clock. Maximum reading of the spring balance $S$ and the rotation of the wheel are also noted down.
5. The RPM $N$ of the wheel is calculated.
6. The BHP is found out by calculation. 
7. Procedure 4,5,6 are repeated thrice and the mean value of BHP is found out.

## Observation and Result 
- Circumference of the brake wheel, $C = \pi D = 1.42\ m$ 
- Diameter of the brake wheel, $D = \frac{C}{\pi} = 1.42/\pi\ m = 0.45\ m$
- Diameter of rope, $d = (0.00522 + 0.00444 + 0.0044)/3\ m = 0.0046\ m$
- Effective radius of the wheel, $R = (D+d)/2 = \frac{0.45 + 0.0046}{2}\ m = 0.23\ m$
