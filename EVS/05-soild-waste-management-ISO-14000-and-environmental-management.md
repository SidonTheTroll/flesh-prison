# Solid Waste Management, ISO 14000 and Environmental Management 
- **Definition of waste:** Waste refers to unwanted or unsuable materials that is discarded after primary use. 

- Types of waste (according to physical state)
    - Solid waste 
    - Liquid waste 
    - Gaseous waste 

- Type of waste 
    1. Biodegradable waste 
    2. Non-biodegradable waste 
    3. Hazardous 
    4. E-waste (electronic waste)
    5. Biomedical waste 
    6. Metallic and non-metallic waste 

## Solid Waste Generation 
- **Municipal Solid Waste (MSW):** it include waste like food packaging, household items, electronics, furniture, construction material, etc. 
- **Domestic Solid Waste:** it includes waste generated from daily activities like kichen waste, vegetable peels, household hazardous waste, etc. 

## Characteristics of MSW (Municipal Solid Waste)
- Moisture content: 20-40% 
- Particle size: from small food scraps to bulky materials 
- Organic matter: high (contains carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen and trace elements)
- Rich in biodegradable content leading to microbial decomposition 

## Electronic Waste (E-Waste)
It refers to discarded electronic equipments like mobile phones, household appliances, batteries, etc. 

### Sources of E-Waste 
1. Large household appliances (refrigerator, AC)
2. Small electronic devices (headphones, mobile phones, charger)
3. IT and Telecommunication (computer, routers, scanner, printer)
4. Lighting equipment (bulbs, lamps)

### Constituents of E-Waste 
Hazardous constituents like lead, mercury, cadmium, polyvinyl chloride (PVC), plastics, and polymer primarily used in electronic equipments pose significant health and environmental risks. 

### Recycling of E-Waste 
E-Waste recycling is the process of extracting valuable materials from discarded electronic appliances. 

- Positive consequences 
    - Preserves natural resources 
    - Minimizing landfill waste 
    - Prevents air, soil and water pollution 
    - Provides economic benefit by creating job opportunities 

- Negative consequences 
    1. Recycling without safety measures exposes workers to toxic chemicals. 
    2. Environmental pollution due to improper recycling. 
    3. Data security risks 
    4. Contamination of food chain 

## Biomedical Waste 
Biomedical waste is any solid or liquid waste generated during the diagnosis, treatment, immunization or health research. 

### 4 Main Categories of Biomedical Waste 
1. **Yellow category:** human and animal waste, body fluids, placenta, bandages, infectious material, etc. 
2. **Red category:** injections, syringes, IV bottles, infected bandages, etc. 
3. **Blue category:** glass items like bottles, broken articles and outdated medicines. 
4. **Black category:** cytotoxic drugs, sharp objects including metals

## Metallic Wastes 
These are solid, non-biodegradable discarded materials primarily generated from industrials processes, manufacturing and construction.  
E.g., electronic devices, vehicle parts, hazardous metals, etc.

### Non-Metallic Waste 
Non-metallic waste comprises both biodegradable and non-biodegradable materials that poses environmental challenges. It comprises of kitchen waste, agricultural waste, single-use plastics, rubber, lubricants, etc. 

- Disposal of Different Types of Solid Waste 
    1. Landfilling 
    2. Inceneration 
    3. Recycling 
    4. Composting 
    5. Waste to energy (WTE) technology
