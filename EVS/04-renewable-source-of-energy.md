# Renewable Source Of Energy 
Energy is the capacity to do work. According to law of conservation of energy, it can neither be created nor be destroyed it can only transform from one type to another.  
Generally, there are two types of energy, renewable, and non-renewable energy. 

- **Renewable Energy:** which can be generated continuously in nature and are inexhaustible. E.g., solar energy, wind energy, tidal energy, geothermal energy, etc.
- **Non-renewable energy:** which can't be quickly regenerated when exhausted. E.g., coal, natural gas, petroleum, etc. 

| Renewable Energy | Non-renewable Energy | 
|-|-|
| These resources can be regenerated again and again within a given span of life. | Resources which can't be regenerated again and again within a given span of life. | 
| The reserves of these resources are unlimited in nature. | Their reserves are limited. | 
| These resources are eco-friendly, i.e., they generally don't cause pollution in the environment. | These resources aren't eco-friendly, i.e., they cause pollution in the environment. | 
| E.g., solar, tidal, geothermal, wind energy, etc. | E.g., coal, petroleum, natural gas, etc. | 

## Why renewable energy is important? 
Conventional energy sources like coal, petroleum, natural gas, etc. are limited in nature. They also cause pollution. Therefore as an alternative of these energy sources, renewable energy is used.  

- Renewable energy: 
    1. Reduces pollution 
    2. Reduces dependence on fossil fuel. 
    3. Helps in sustainable development (development that meets present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs).
    4. It is available for long term use. 

## Advantage of Renewable Energy Sources 
1. They are eco-friendly
2. They don't produce mass pollution 
3. They reduce the use of non-renewable fuels
4. The help in sustainable development 

## Disadvantage of Renewable Energy Sources 
1. Some sources depend on climate and weather 
2. Initial installation cost may be high 
3. Some system needs large land area 
4. Energy production may be low due to weather conditions. 

# Solar Energy 
It is the energy received from sun in the form of radiation. The sun is the largest source of energy for the earth. Sunlight contains heat and light energy which can be converted into useful forms such as heat energy, electrical energy, mechanical energy, etc.  
Solar energy is the most useful renewable energy because it is free, abundant and non-polluting. 

## Principle of Solar Energy Conversion 
When sunlight falls on a surface, the surface absorbs the radiation and converts it into heat. This heat can be used directly or can be used to produce electricity with the help of solar cells. 

> Q/ Why solar energy is the most useful renewable source?  
> Solar energy is the most useful renewable energy source because it is available everywhere, solar panels can be installed in basic infrastructure like roofs, technologically harnessable and can be easily integrated into energy grid, furthermore, it is non-polluting and unlimited. 

## Flat Plate Collector 
It is a device used to collect solar heat and transfer into water or air. It works without tracking the sun. The main parts of flat plate collector are: 

1. Glass cover 
2. Absorber plate 
3. Turbine or air passage 
4. Insulation material 
5. Outer frame 

### Working of Flat Plate Collector 
1. Sunlight enters throug glass cover 
2. Absorber plate gets heated 
3. Heat transfers to the water or air flowing in tubes 
4. Hot water collected in the storage tank 

### Limitations of Flat Plate Collector 
1. Depends on sun 
2. High initial cost 
3. Lower output in rainy season 

# Biomass 
Biomass is the organic matter obtained from plant and animal waste, which is used to produce energy. E.g., food waste, cow dung, crop waste, etc.  
Biomass is carbon neutral because the carbon dioxide released during burning is nearly equal to the carbon dioxide absorbed by plants during their growth. 

## Advantages of Biomass 
1. They are renewable energy source 
2. They use waste materials 
3. They reduce pollution 
4. They are useful in rural area 

## Thermal Characteristics of Biomass 
A good biomass should have 

1. High caloritic value 
2. Low moisture content 
3. Low ash content 

## Anaerobic Digestion 
Anaerobic digestion is the breakdown or decomposition of organic matter by microorganisms in absence of oxygen. It produces biogas. The gas produce from anaerobic digestion of biomass is called biogas.  
The composition of biogas are: 

1. Methane (50-70%)
2. Carbon dioxide (30-40%)
3. Small traces of $H_2S,\ N$ and water vapor. 

## Properties of Biogas 
1. It is a colorless gas 
2. Burns with blue flame 
3. It is a clean fuel 
4. The main combustible gas is methane 

# Wind Energy 
It is the energy obtained from moving air. Wind rotates the turbine and converts wind energy into electrical energy. 

## Advantages of Wind Energy 
1. It is renewable energy 
2. It is free energy 
3. It is a clean source of energy
